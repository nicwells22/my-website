# Personal Brand Guide

## Brand Essence

This personal brand is inspired by a high-alpine winter landscape: clear blue sky, rugged mountain rock, clean snowfields, evergreen forests, and relaxed outdoor confidence. The style should feel grounded, capable, adventurous, and calm.

## Brand Positioning

**Core idea:** Practical expertise with an explorer's mindset.

**Brand personality:**

- **Grounded:** Confident without being loud.
- **Adventurous:** Open to challenge, motion, and discovery.
- **Clear:** Direct communication with crisp structure.
- **Resilient:** Built around endurance, problem-solving, and trust.
- **Approachable:** Casual, human, and easy to connect with.

## Visual Direction

The image suggests a brand world defined by contrast: bright snow against deep trees, crisp sky against rugged stone, and relaxed people against a dramatic landscape. Use generous whitespace, strong contrast, and natural textures.

**Design keywords:**

- Alpine
- Crisp
- Rugged
- Minimal
- Trustworthy
- Outdoors-oriented
- Calm confidence

## Recommended Color Usage

- **Primary combination:** `#F3F6F8` background with `#223A5E` text and `#4E78AD` accents.
- **Dark mode combination:** `#1F332D` or `#223A5E` background with `#F3F6F8` text.
- **Accent hierarchy:** Use `#B59A67` for warmth and `#D85F2A` only when something needs immediate attention.
- **Avoid:** Overusing orange, neon colors, heavy gradients, or overly polished corporate blues.

## Typography

Use typography that feels technical, clean, and outdoors-capable.

**Recommended pairing:**

- **Headings:** `Inter`, `Satoshi`, `Avenir Next`, or `Montserrat`
- **Body:** `Inter`, `Source Sans 3`, `system-ui`, or `Arial`
- **Code / technical details:** `JetBrains Mono`, `Fira Code`, or `SFMono-Regular`

**Type style:**

- Headings should be bold, compact, and clear.
- Body text should be readable and unpretentious.
- Avoid ornate, luxury, handwritten, or overly playful fonts.

## Logo and Mark Direction

A strong mark could combine one or more of these ideas:

- **Mountain ridge line:** Simple angular peak silhouette.
- **Trail marker:** A vertical post or waypoint shape.
- **Summit initials:** Personal initials integrated into a mountain or compass form.
- **Snow line:** A clean horizontal or diagonal break suggesting elevation.

The mark should work in one color first, especially in `#223A5E`, `#F3F6F8`, or `#1F332D`.

## Photography Style

Use imagery that feels real, expansive, and natural.

**Preferred imagery:**

- Mountain landscapes
- Snow, rock, sky, and evergreen textures
- Outdoor group moments
- Candid confidence rather than staged posing
- Wide compositions with scale and open space

**Editing style:**

- Crisp contrast
- Natural blues and whites
- Slightly cool temperature
- Preserve texture in snow, rock, and trees
- Avoid heavy filters or oversaturation

## Layout System

The layout should feel spacious and structured, like a clear route through a large landscape.

**Principles:**

- Use generous top and bottom spacing.
- Prefer strong horizontal sections.
- Use cards sparingly and keep them clean.
- Let whitespace carry the design.
- Use thin dividers in `#B9C5D3` rather than heavy borders.
- Use dark hero or footer sections for contrast.

## UI Components

**Buttons:**

- Primary buttons: `#4E78AD` background with `#F3F6F8` text.
- Secondary buttons: transparent with `#223A5E` border and text.
- Highlight buttons: `#B59A67` background for warmer, human calls to action.

**Cards:**

- Background: `#FFFFFF` or `#F3F6F8`
- Border: `#B9C5D3`
- Shadow: subtle, cool-toned, low opacity
- Radius: moderate, around `12px` to `18px`

**Links:**

- Default: `#4E78AD`
- Hover: `#223A5E`
- Active or important: `#D85F2A`

## Voice and Tone

The written voice should be confident, concise, and practical.

**Tone attributes:**

- **Direct:** Say what matters quickly.
- **Capable:** Show competence through clarity, not exaggeration.
- **Curious:** Frame work as exploration and continuous improvement.
- **Human:** Keep language natural and relaxed.
- **Reliable:** Make promises carefully and follow through.

## Messaging Examples

**Short tagline options:**

- Build clearly. Move confidently.
- Practical systems for ambitious terrain.
- Calm execution in complex environments.
- Grounded thinking for high-altitude goals.
- Clear strategy. Rugged follow-through.

**Bio direction:**

> I bring a clear, grounded approach to complex work: mapping the terrain, choosing the right route, and building practical systems that hold up under real conditions.

**Call-to-action examples:**

- Start the conversation
- Explore the work
- Plan the route
- Build something durable

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Use crisp blue, snow white, evergreen, and granite neutrals.
- Keep layouts open and breathable.
- Use real imagery with natural scale.
- Communicate with calm authority.
- Let ruggedness appear through texture, contrast, and simplicity.

**Don't:**

- Use flashy gradients or neon palettes.
- Make the brand feel overly corporate or sterile.
- Overload pages with dense visual elements.
- Use adventure language so heavily that it becomes gimmicky.
- Sacrifice readability for atmosphere.
